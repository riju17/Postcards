About the Bird

With a slender cinnamon neck, chestnut mane, and slate-blue wings, the Purple Heron melts into reed beds better than any marsh hunter. Its patient stalking of fish, frogs, and dragonflies has made it an enduring subject of wetland stamp issues from India to the Netherlands.

These herons nest deep within floating reed islands, often sharing space with ibises and storks. Their reliance on intact marshes makes them ideal ambassadors for Ramsar sites and monsoon conservation campaigns, giving philatelic collectors plenty of reasons to celebrate this elegant predator.
