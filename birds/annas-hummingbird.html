About the Bird

Anna’s Hummingbird thrives in Pacific coastal gardens, city balconies, and eucalyptus groves where winter blooms still offer nectar. Known for the dazzling magenta gorget of the males, they flare those feathers like sequins whenever sunlight hits just right, inspiring postcard artists to capture their neon flashes.

They are hardy pollinators, enduring cooler climates longer than most hummingbird species. Their ability to nest early in the year and defend hummingbird feeders endears them to backyard naturalists, making them a frequent subject of maxim cards and special cancellations commemorating urban wildlife corridors.
